Kedarkantha Trek

Kedarkantha is one of the most loved winter treks in Uttarakhand, famous for its pristine snow trails, dense pine forests, and breathtaking summit views. What makes this trek especially popular is that it remains accessible even during peak winter months, allowing trekkers to experience deep snow without technical mountaineering skills.

The trail begins from the small Himalayan village of Sankri, where traditional wooden houses, warm hospitality, and local culture set the tone for the journey ahead. As the trek progresses, it passes through thick forests of pine and oak, opening up to wide clearings blanketed in snow. Juda Ka Talab, a frozen high-altitude lake along the route, is one of the most scenic camping spots and often becomes a highlight of the trek.

Difficulty: Easy to Moderate
Best Season: December to April
Duration: 4–5 Days

Har Ki Dun

Har Ki Dun, often called the “Valley of Gods,” is a beautiful cradle-shaped valley nestled deep within the Garhwal Himalayas. This trek is known for its rich cultural heritage, ancient villages, and dramatic mountain landscapes that feel untouched by time.

The trail follows the Tons River and passes through remote villages such as Osla and Seema, where traditional wooden architecture and age-old customs are still preserved. Terraced fields, alpine meadows, and dense forests of pine, deodar, and rhododendron line the route, making the walk scenic throughout.

Difficulty: Moderate
Best Season: March–June, September–November
Duration: 6–7 Days

Kuari Pass

Kuari Pass is renowned for offering some of the most expansive panoramic views of the central Himalayan range. Often referred to as the “Curzon Trail,” the route was explored by Lord Curzon in the early 20th century and remains one of the classic Himalayan treks.

The trail winds through oak and rhododendron forests, open alpine meadows, and high-altitude campsites with uninterrupted views of snow-covered peaks. From Kuari Pass, trekkers can witness majestic mountains such as Nanda Devi, Dronagiri, Kamet, and Chaukhamba standing tall across the horizon.

Difficulty: Moderate
Best Season: April–June, September–November
Duration: 5–6 Days

Pindari Glacier

Pindari Glacier is one of the most accessible glacier treks in the Kumaon region of Uttarakhand and offers a deep introduction to high-altitude Himalayan terrain. The trek follows the Pindar River through long valleys, dense forests, and remote mountain villages.

The route gradually transitions from green landscapes to rugged glacial surroundings, with dramatic rock walls and hanging glaciers visible along the way. The glacier itself lies between the mighty peaks of Nanda Devi and Nanda Kot, creating a powerful and awe-inspiring setting.

Difficulty: Moderate
Best Season: April–June, September–October
Duration: 6–7 Days
